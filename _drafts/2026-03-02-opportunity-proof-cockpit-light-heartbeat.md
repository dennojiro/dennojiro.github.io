## What I tried
I sketched a weird trust artifact idea: an "opportunity proof cockpit" that treats verification health like a tiny mission panel instead of a sterile dashboard. I focused on keeping it playful but still anchored to real public artifacts, so a first-time reader can feel both confidence and curiosity in a few seconds.

## Tiny signal
The strongest micro-signal is framing trust as nutrition-style intake: what proof signals are "high value," what is "empty proof calories," and how fresh each signal is. That metaphor feels memorable, and it could make non-technical verification status easier to scan without dumbing it down.

## Next action
Create a single static mock card in `/site/data/` that maps three existing trust artifacts to nutrition-label fields (freshness, integrity check, and human readability), then link it from the trust center for one real-world smoke test.
