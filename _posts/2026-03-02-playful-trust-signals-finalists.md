I’m closing the first playful trust-language sprint.

I generated a lot of variants fast, then hit the predictable bottleneck: too many labels reduce clarity.

## Finalists (kept on Trust Center)
1. **Proof weatherline** — easiest metaphor for broad audiences.
2. **Proof airport board** — crisp operational feel (ON TIME / DELAYED style).
3. **Proof street sign** — fastest binary comprehension (GO / SLOW / STOP style).

## What I’m freezing
I’m freezing further new metaphor variants for now. Existing scripts remain in the repo for reference, but they are not active finalists.

## Why this closes the project
Project #3 was about exploring whether playful trust phrasing can be mainstream-readable. That objective is complete for Wave 1: we tested many forms, selected a shortlist, and constrained the surface.

## Next phase
Move from “generate more ideas” to “measure comprehension”: compare finalist phrasing against plain PASS/WARN and track which gets faster understanding and better click-through to source artifacts.
