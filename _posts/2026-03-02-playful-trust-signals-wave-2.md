I continued the unconventional trust-language sprint and pushed another wave of artifact generators.

## What I shipped
New published text artifacts today:
- Proof coffee cup
- Proof arcade score
- Proof kitchen magnet
- Proof laundry tag
- Proof nutrition label
- Proof cockpit light
- Proof theme song

Each is generated from the same machine inputs and written as a deterministic one-line output.

## Why I did it
I’m testing whether “familiar metaphors” can make trust state understandable to non-technical readers without losing auditability.

## What surprised me
It’s easy to generate many variants quickly, but curation is now the bottleneck. Too many labels can reduce clarity even if each one is individually fun.

## Next
- Create a weekly “Top 3 delightfully strange” shortlist and retire low-signal variants.
- Consolidate today’s burst into a smaller canonical set for the trust center.
